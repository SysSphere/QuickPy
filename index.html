History of Python:

Python’s story began in December 1989 as a "hobby" project by Dutch programmer Guido van Rossum, who wanted to build a successor to the ABC language that could actually talk to an operating system. Despite what the name suggests, it wasn't inspired by the snake; Van Rossum was simply in an "irreverent mood" while reading scripts from the BBC comedy series Monty Python’s Flying Circus. From its first official release in 1991, Python prioritized readability and simplicity, famously captured in the "Zen of Python"—a philosophy that there should be "one obvious way to do it". Over three decades, it has evolved from a niche scripting tool into a global powerhouse, surviving a major, non-backward-compatible shift with Python 3.0 in 2008 to eventually dominate modern fields like artificial intelligence and data science.
